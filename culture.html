French Culinary Culture

Dining Etiquette

Keep hands visible on the table (wrists resting on the edge) and wait for your host to say “Bon appétit” before starting your meal. Bread is typically placed directly on the tablecloth to the left of your plate, not on a separate bread plate.

Food Festivals

France hosts numerous food festivals year-round. The Fête de la Gastronomie, typically held in September, is a nationwide event celebrating regional specialties, culinary arts, and the French "art de vivre."

Traditional Methods

Classic techniques like braising (mijoter), confit (preserving in fat), and cooking en croute (in a pastry crust) emphasize slow cooking and layering flavors to achieve richness and depth.
